About This Course

Course Overview

This intensive 5-day course covers the full dynamics of the international gas industry — from market fundamentals and reserve estimation through to the economics of gas projects and investment decision analysis. It provides a rigorous, practical toolkit for evaluating gas projects under uncertainty.

The programme combines market analysis with hands-on Excel-based economic modelling, covering NPV, IRR, capital budgeting and risk analysis. Participants build and run their own economic models and work through case studies from real global gas projects.

Classroom sessions are held in 2026 at five-star hotel venues in Bali, Rio de Janeiro, Singapore and Doha — four of the world's most important gas market hubs.

Programme Content

Key Topics

01

Global Gas Markets & Supply-Demand Fundamentals

  • Overview of the global natural gas industry and market structure
  • Nature of natural gas: reserves, resources and estimation methods
  • Petroleum Resources Management System (PRMS) framework
  • Global LNG trade flows, pricing mechanisms and market evolution
  • Impact of US shale gas and LNG on global gas market dynamics
02

Shale Gas Economics & Development

  • Geology and technical characteristics of unconventional gas resources
  • Economics of shale gas: cost structure, decline curves and breakevens
  • Impact of shale gas on LNG market pricing and trade patterns
  • Regulatory and environmental considerations for shale development
  • Case studies: US, Canada, Australia and emerging shale markets
03

Gas Project Economics & Investment Analysis

  • Time value of money, cash-flow models and capital budgeting
  • NPV, IRR, payback period and breakeven price calculations
  • Building gas project economic evaluation models in Excel
  • Risk and uncertainty analysis: Monte Carlo, decision trees and sensitivity
  • Reducing exposure and mitigating project risk in gas investments
04

Gas Contracts, Pricing & Commercial Structures

  • Gas Sales Agreement (GSA) structures and key commercial terms
  • Gas pricing mechanisms: oil-linked, hub-based and hybrid approaches
  • LNG SPA structures: long-term vs spot and portfolio optimisation
  • Midstream and downstream commercial models in gas projects
  • Fiscal terms and government take in gas development projects
05

Decision Analysis & Strategic Opportunities

  • Maximising investment returns through sound decision-making
  • Portfolio analysis: ranking and prioritising gas project opportunities
  • Commercial viability assessment and go/no-go decision frameworks
  • Hands-on: building and solving your own gas project economic model
  • Emerging opportunities: energy transition, hydrogen and gas-to-power
