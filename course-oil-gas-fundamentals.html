About This Course

Course Overview

This 3-day oil and gas fundamentals course gives participants a clear, practical overview of how the oil and gas business works from exploration to end-user. Technical concepts are explained in plain language and connected directly to commercial decisions, project economics and fiscal frameworks.

Participants will understand how upstream, midstream, downstream and petrochemicals fit together, how energy companies generate value at each stage, and how fiscal and regulatory terms affect project returns. Ideal for professionals who work alongside technical or commercial teams and need a solid industry grounding.

Classroom sessions are held in 2026 at five-star hotel venues in London (March), Dubai (May), Calgary (July) and Kuala Lumpur (October).

Programme Content

Key Topics

01

The Oil & Gas Value Chain: An Overview

  • How the global oil and gas industry is structured
  • Upstream, midstream, downstream and petrochemicals: roles and interactions
  • Key players: NOCs, IOCs, independents, service companies and traders
  • How oil and gas companies make money at each stage of the chain
  • Energy transition context: how the industry is changing
02

Upstream: Exploration, Drilling & Production

  • Basics of petroleum geology: traps, reservoirs and source rocks
  • Exploration techniques: seismic surveys and well logging
  • Drilling: onshore vs offshore, well types and completion
  • Field development planning and production optimisation
  • Natural gas production, processing and transportation basics
03

Midstream, Downstream & Commercial Structures

  • Crude oil transportation: pipelines, tankers and storage
  • Oil refining: distillation basics and key refined products
  • Natural gas processing: NGL extraction, LNG and gas transmission
  • Trading: how crude oil and products are bought and sold globally
  • Petroleum contracts, royalties, taxes and project economics
Learning Outcomes

What You Will Learn

01Understand the full oil and gas value chain from exploration and drilling to refining, trading and end-use
02Explain the basics of petroleum geology, exploration, drilling and field development
03Describe how crude oil and natural gas are produced, processed, transported and marketed
04Recognise how petroleum contracts, royalties, taxes and PSCs affect project returns
05Interpret key financial indicators such as NPV, IRR, breakeven price and netbacks
06Discuss major risks, energy transition drivers and their impact on the oil and gas business
